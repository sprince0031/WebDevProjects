About the College

R.M.K. Engineering College is a NAAC accredited and ISO certified institution established by Shri. R.S. Munirathinam. The college is situated in a sylvan environment and offers Under Graduate and Post Graduate courses in Engineering, Technology, Management and Computer Applications. The institution has grown greatly and is recognized among the reputable institutions in Tamil Nadu within a short span of time because of the highest degree of commitment and enthusiasm of the Staff and Management. The institution disseminates knowledge and entrepreneurial skills among the students and strives to achieve excellence in the field of technical, computer and management education. The college is one among the top colleges in Tamil Nadu and ranked 84th among all IIT's ,NIT's and other top institutions in the nation by NIRF, conducted by MHRD

About the Department

The Department of Civil Engineering was established in the year 2006 with a sanctioned intake of 40 students. Introducing various advancements throughout the years, the department now has a capacity of 120 students. The department aims to enrich the knowledge of the students in both theory and practice with experiences in the diversified streams of civil engineering. It plays a vital role in training the students through in-plant training and Industrial visits to overcome the field associated problems arising in the industry. The department actively involves in research activities and industrial consultancy works with various firms and industries in and around the city. The department also organizes a number of technical events on latest advancements in civil engineering, every year for the upgradation and enhancement of knowledge of the students.

About Colosseum

The National level technical symposium conducted by the department of Civil Engineering. The name of this symposium is derived from a italic monument called colloseum, and the main motto of our symposium is to trigger the technical intellectual skills present in the student community. Every year our department is organizing this National Level Technical Symposium in a very grand manner for the benefit of student community. We here in COLLOSEUM'18 rebuilt are exclusively conducting technical events like Paper Presentation, Technical Quiz, and other surprising Events. This symposium is an excellent opportunity for all the students to exhibit their technical knowledge and win great prizes and it is a thought provoking event that lets all to learn many things about the current trends in Civil Engineering.

Verde Katidam

Green building is the practice of creating structures and using processes that are environmentally responsible and resource-efficient throughout a building's life-cycle from siting to design, construction, operation, maintenance, renovation and deconstruction. This day and age , you hear everyone talking about going green. Whether you want to admit it or not , at some point everyone will have to follow with the green movement. This is because at the rate we are going, the earth is simply not sustainable. That means that over the years, we will being to run out of certain natural resources that are needed in order for us to survive. That is alarming to some people, which is why there are so many people that focus on green building. Green buildings promise to be a great source to reduce the overall carbon footprint. There by we come up with idea of Indian Green Building Challenge.

Description :

The event INDIAN GREEN BUILDING CHALLENGE is based on the strategies to achieve

  • Reduce energy consumption
  • Water conservation
  • Recycling waste

EVENT STRUCTURE :

Round-I
Teams will be given with the statement specifications and conditions. Based on the statement specifications the teams have to give the plan of the building in the stipulated time.Six Teams will be shortlisted for the round-II based on the best plan, area usage efficiency and the time taken.
Round-II
The shortlisted six teams have to come up with the comparison of materials used and budget analysis to your green building and conventional building. There by how you would market your green building. The team which is best in analysis and marketing will be the winner.

Rules & Regulations :

  • Team should consist of 2-3 members.
  • Time for the first round will be 20 minutes.
  • Access to gadgets and internet will not be provided.

Fix It

Here’s an event to manifest how ready you are to put an end to the proliferating real time problems faced by the civil engineers. The solution that you provide should be feasible and durable such that it magnifies your credentials. In the march towards creating a sustainable environment let this be the podium for you to come up with efficient solutions.

ABOUT THE EVENT:

The event consists of two rounds.
ROUND 1:
Two problem statements pertaining to the real time problems faced in civil engineering will be given.
The team should come up with the measures that can be taken to overcome those difficulties and also solutions to implement those measures. Refer to the problem statements below. Maximum of 5 minutes can be taken for explaining.
Top 5 teams will be selected for the second round.
ROUND 2:
In this round the participants will be given with a case study. This case study can be about an ongoing project or synopsis of some calamities that occurred. These problems are to be analysed in civil engineering point of view and the participants need to crop up with feasible solutions. This will an on the spot round. 10 to 15 minutes time will be given for discussion and 5 minutes for presentation.
Top two solutions will be awarded accordingly.

PROBLEM STATEMENTS:

  • Problem statement 1:

    • Kerala - God’s own country is now drowning in the monsoon floods just after the flood ruckus in the North East. It is not just a sudden wrath of the monsoons. The occurrence of severe floods has become the new norm in recent times (How can we forget the Chennai floods!). Dams have been engineering marvels that protected people from both droughts and floods. Sadly now dams aren’t enough to save us. As responsible civil engineers we have a duty to bring in innovation and address the problem of the present. Keeping in mind the drastically changing climatic and environmental conditions, develop a solution that shall stand the test of time.

  • Problem statement 2:

    • Oceans are the most bountiful resources that nature has gifted us. The potentially infinite resources in the dark depths of the ocean could sustain lives for nearly forever. But looking at the sad reality of these precious gifts it leads one to wonder that maybe we humans do not deserve so much of kindness. The oceans have lost their sparkle due to our negligence. Oil spilling, effluent discharges from industries, careless deep ocean drilling, indiscriminate dumping of plastics are rendering the oceans useless. The abundant life inside the ocean is crying out for help. This cry when heard by a civil engineer could possibly solve the menace. Let us work our brains and come up with solutions to give back the future what the nature gave us, in its original glory.

RULES:

  • Maximum of 3 members in a team.
  • The teams should have a team name.
  • Time limit should be adhered properly.
  • Electronic gadgets should not be used.
  • Participants are allowed to bring models or posters for effective explanation.
